Title: Alarm frequency by day of week
Legend: Box plots showing the distribution of daily alarm counts for each day of the week over the 57-day study period (July 15 – September 9, 2025). [Figure 2] fig2_hourly_alarm_type
Title: Hourly alarm burden by alarm type
Legend: Stacked bar chart showing the average number of alarms per hour of the day, stratified by alarm type. Blue bars represent clinical alarms (physiological events such as SpO2 desaturation, tachycardia, and arrhythmias); yellow bars represent technical alarms (device or signal issues such as probe disconnection, electrode detachment, and signal loss). Values represent daily averages over the 57-day study period.

## fig_heatmap_clinical_by_dow
Title: Clinical Alarms by Hour of Day and Day of Week
Legend:
  Heatmap showing the distribution of clinical alarms across hours of the day (x-axis)
  and days of the week (y-axis, Monday to Sunday). Each cell represents the total alarm
  count over the 57-day observation period (2025/7/15 – 9/9). Three panels correspond
  to alarm priority levels: ADVISORY (green), WARNING (yellow), and CRISIS (red).
  Only clinical alarms (alarm_class == 'clinical') are included.

## fig_heatmap_clinical_by_zone
Title: Clinical Alarms by Hour of Day and Day of Week (Stratified by Zone)
Legend:
  Six-panel heatmap comparing clinical alarm distribution between high-acuity zone
  (12 beds: 1101-A to E, Re-A/B, W10-01 to 05) and general zone (29 beds).
  Rows represent priority levels (ADVISORY, WARNING, CRISIS); columns represent zones.
  X-axis: hour of day (0-23); Y-axis: day of week (Monday to Sunday).
  Color intensity indicates alarm count per cell (hour × day combination) over the
  57-day observation period. Color scale is unified within each priority row to allow
  direct zone comparison.

Figure [X]: Alarm silencing rate by alarm burden quartile and priority level

Observed silencing rates (%) with 95% Wilson confidence intervals are shown
for each combination of alarm burden quartile (Q1–Q4, preceding 30 minutes)
and alarm priority (ADVISORY, WARNING, CRISIS).
Alarm burden quartiles: Q1 = 0–9, Q2 = 9–18, Q3 = 18–31, Q4 = 31–97 alarms/30 min.
N = 59,113 clinical alarms.
Error bars represent 95% Wilson confidence intervals.
